Description

Many games, like chess and Go!, are based on square grids. Hip is no exception, but it adds a unique twist (literally).
The Game of Hip is a 2 player game created by Martin Gardner in 1960, so named because of hipsters' reputed disdain for 'squares.' It is played on a checkerboard as follows:
Each player has different colored pieces. They take turns placing a single piece on any vacant cell of the board. Each tries to avoid placing his pieces so that four of them mark the corners of a square. The square may be any size and tipped at any angle. A player wins when his opponent becomes a 'square' by forming a square with his pieces.
It has been shown that for a 6×6 board and smaller there is possibility of a tie, however for 7×7 and larger it is not possible to tie. For a 6×6 board there are 105 different possible squares.
In 2014 things have changed for hipsters, and now they actually like squares. They have changed the game to allow more players. The winner is the player with the most squares on the board once there are no tiles left. Their problem is that it is difficult for them to count up how many squares they each have, so they need your help. Given a board with tiles on it, you need to tell them how many squares each color has.
